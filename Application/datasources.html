DATA SOURCES

The Open Aid Map captures and visualizes development assistance data as reported by Governments through country aid information management systems and as reported by donors. Geocoded development projects reported by donors are derived from the IATI registry and open data portals of each development agency. Poverty, demographic, and other development indicators are based on reliable, public information gathered from national statistical offices, government ministries, and international organizations, agencies and programs, such as the World Bank, WHO and Measure DHS.

Geographic boundaries

The boundaries, colors, denominations and any other information shown on this map do not imply, on the part of The World Bank Group, any judgment on the legal status of any territory, or any endorsement or acceptance of such boundaries.

    Bolivia

    Project data:

    The content on this page is not in English as it is directly derived from the database of the country's national database. The Bolivia map is based on the data of development projects as reported in the Sistema de Informaci ón Sobre Inversiones (SISIN), Ministry of Planning and Development, Plurinational State of Bolivia, 2013. The data is being presented in its original language, Spanish, with the exception of sector information, which follows sector taxonomy of the International Aid Transparency Initiative (IATI).

    Socioeconomic indicators:

    Poverty Headcount (%, 2001): Proportion of the population living on less than US$1 a day, measured at 2001 international prices, adjusted for purchasing power parity (PPP).
    Source: Instituto Nacional de Estadisticas (INE) / Encuesta de Hogares (National Institute of Statistics)

    Total Population (2001 and 2010): Total population is based on the de facto definition of population, which counts all residents regardless of legal status or citizenship, except for refugees not permanently settled in the country of asylum, who are generally considered part of the population of their country of origin.
    Source: Ministry of Planning and Development, Bolivia
